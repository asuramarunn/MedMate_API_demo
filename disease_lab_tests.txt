# Disease and associated lab tests with typical indicator values/ranges for diagnosis

Community-acquired pneumonia:
- Complete Blood Count (CBC):
    - WBC: Elevated (>10,000 /µL)
    - Neutrophils: Increased percentage
- C-Reactive Protein (CRP):
    - CRP: Elevated (>10 mg/L)
- Chest X-ray:
    - Finding: Infiltrates or consolidation
- Sputum Gram Stain:
    - Presence of pathogens (e.g. Streptococcus pneumoniae)
- Blood Culture:
    - Positive for causative bacteria

Acute myocardial infarction:
- Troponin I:
    - Elevated above 99th percentile upper reference limit
- ECG:
    - ST segment elevation or depression, pathological Q waves
- Creatine Kinase-MB (CK-MB):
    - Elevated (>5 ng/mL)
- Complete Blood Count (CBC):
    - May show leukocytosis

Pulmonary embolism:
- D-Dimer:
    - Elevated (>500 ng/mL)
- Chest X-ray:
    - Often normal or nonspecific (e.g. atelectasis)
- CT Pulmonary Angiography:
    - Filling defects in pulmonary arteries
- Arterial Blood Gas (ABG):
    - Hypoxemia (PaO2 <80 mmHg)
    - Respiratory alkalosis (low PaCO2)

Sepsis:
- Complete Blood Count (CBC):
    - Leukocytosis or leukopenia
    - Left shift (immature neutrophils)
- C-Reactive Protein (CRP):
    - Elevated
- Blood Culture:
    - Positive for pathogens
- Procalcitonin (PCT):
    - Elevated (>0.5 ng/mL)
- Lactate:
    - Elevated (>2 mmol/L), indicates tissue hypoperfusion

Chronic Kidney Disease:
- Serum Creatinine:
    - Elevated (e.g. >1.2 mg/dL in females, >1.4 mg/dL in males)
- Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN):
    - Elevated (>20 mg/dL)
- Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR):
    - Reduced (<60 mL/min/1.73 m²)
- Urinalysis:
    - Proteinuria, hematuria

Liver Cirrhosis:
- Liver Function Tests (LFT):
    - Elevated AST and ALT (variable)
    - Elevated alkaline phosphatase (ALP)
- Albumin:
    - Decreased (<3.5 g/dL)
- Bilirubin:
    - Elevated (>1.2 mg/dL)
- Prothrombin Time (PT):
    - Prolonged

Diabetes Mellitus:
- Fasting Blood Glucose:
    - Elevated (>126 mg/dL)
- HbA1c:
    - Elevated (>6.5%)
- Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT):
    - 2-hour plasma glucose >200 mg/dL
- Urine Microalbumin:
    - Elevated (>30 mg/g creatinine)

Thyroid Disorders:
- TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone):
    - Hypothyroidism: Elevated
    - Hyperthyroidism: Suppressed
- Free T4:
    - Hypothyroidism: Low
    - Hyperthyroidism: Elevated
- Free T3:
    - Similar pattern as Free T4
- Thyroid Antibodies:
    - Positive in autoimmune thyroid disease

Rheumatoid Arthritis:
- Rheumatoid Factor (RF):
    - Positive (>20 IU/mL)
- Anti-CCP Antibodies:
    - Positive
- Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR):
    - Elevated
- C-Reactive Protein (CRP):
    - Elevated
